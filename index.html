About

Researching how robots can move more naturally in human social spaces

I earned my Ph.D. in Human-Robot Interaction from Kyoto University in September 2025, after completing an M.A. in Social Informatics at Kyoto University and a B.Sc. in Computer Science at China University of Geosciences, Beijing.

My research centers on robot navigation and interaction in public environments, especially suggestive avoidance, repeated obstruction prevention, and human-inspired responses to sudden robot stops.

Selected Research

Representative work on social robot navigation and interaction

HRI 2025

Human-Inspired Solutions for Sudden Robot Stops

Investigates how people react when a robot stops unexpectedly and proposes behavior strategies that reduce awkwardness, blockage, and breakdowns in interaction.

Contact for details

HRI 2024

Suggestive Avoidance for Robots

Explores how robots can signal “please give way” through motion, making avoidance behavior more natural, efficient, and legible to surrounding people.

Publication info

Sensors 2020

Recognition of Rare Low-Moral Actions Using Depth Data

Uses depth data to identify rare but socially meaningful actions, contributing to the understanding of unusual behavior and social norms in public settings.
